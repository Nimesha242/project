Beautiful Places

Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura, located in the North Central Province of Sri Lanka, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. It served as the island's capital for over a millennium until the 11th century. Renowned for its ancient Buddhist stupas, dagobas, and monastic ruins, Anuradhapura is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, attracting pilgrims and tourists alike. The sacred Bodhi Tree, a descendant of the tree under which Buddha attained enlightenment, is a major pilgrimage site. The city's archaeological treasures, such as the Ruwanwelisaya and Thuparamaya, reflect its rich cultural and religious history, making it a significant destination for those interested in Sri Lanka's past.

kandy

Kandy, located in the central part of Sri Lanka, is a city steeped in history and cultural significance. It served as the last capital of the ancient Sinhalese kings and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The city is surrounded by lush hills and a picturesque lake. The Temple of the Tooth Relic (Sri Dalada Maligawa) is a focal point, housing the sacred tooth relic of Buddha. The Esala Perahera, a grand annual procession, showcases traditional dances, music, and adorned elephants, drawing visitors from around the world. Kandy seamlessly blends its historical charm with natural beauty, making it a captivating destination in Sri Lanka.

Polonnaruwa

Polonnaruwa, situated in the North Central Province of Sri Lanka, is another UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its well-preserved ancient ruins. It was the second capital of the country after the destruction of Anuradhapura and served as the seat of government between the 11th and 13th centuries. The city boasts impressive archaeological treasures, including the Gal Vihara, a rock temple with stunning Buddha statues, and the Royal Palace complex. The Parakrama Samudra, a vast ancient reservoir, reflects the advanced engineering of its time. Polonnaruwa provides a glimpse into Sri Lanka's medieval history and is a significant cultural and historical destination for visitors.

Sigiriya

Sigiriya, also known as the Lion Rock, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the central Matale District of Sri Lanka. This iconic rock fortress stands majestically, rising 200 meters above the surrounding landscape. Built in the 5th century by King Kashyapa, Sigiriya served as a royal palace and later became a Buddhist monastery. The site is renowned for its ancient frescoes, depicting celestial maidens, and the impressive Lion's Gate entrance. Visitors can climb to the summit to explore the ruins of the palace complex and enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. Sigiriya is a symbol of Sri Lanka's rich cultural and historical heritage.

Jaffna

Jaffna, in northern Sri Lanka, is a city marked by its rich Tamil heritage and post-conflict rebuilding. Home to the Nallur Kandaswamy Kovil and the historic Jaffna Fort, the city encapsulates a blend of vibrant culture and resilience. Emerging from the shadow of a prolonged civil conflict, Jaffna is reclaiming its identity with renewed energy. The reconstructed Jaffna Public Library stands as a symbol of cultural revival. The region's unique cuisine and picturesque islands, like Delft, contribute to Jaffna's allure. As the city heals and opens up, it beckons visitors to witness its historical charm and the enduring spirit of its people.

Nuwara-Eliya

Nuwara Eliya, often referred to as "Little England," is a charming hill station nestled in the central highlands of Sri Lanka. Known for its cool climate, lush tea plantations, and colonial architecture, it provides a stark contrast to the tropical landscapes elsewhere in the country. The town is surrounded by picturesque mountains, waterfalls, and serene lakes. Horton Plains National Park, with its World's End escarpment, is a short drive away. Nuwara Eliya is a hub for Sri Lanka's tea industry, and visitors can explore the scenic tea estates and learn about the tea-making process. The city's Victorian-era structures and the scenic Gregory Lake contribute to its unique appeal.
